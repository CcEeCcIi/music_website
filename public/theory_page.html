The Four Periods of Music History

  1. The Baroque Period(1600 - 1750)

    Keyboard music of the Baroque period was often written with each hand playing its own melody or "voice".

      Important composers:
    • Johann Sebastian Bach (Germany, 1685 - 1750)

      Bach's picture is from: https://www.biography.com/musician/johann-sebastian-bach
    • Georg Philipp Telemann (Germany, 1681 - 1767)
    • Jean Philippe Rameau (France, 1683 - 1764)

  2. The Classical Period (1750 - 1825)

    Piano music of the Classical period was often written with the right hand playing a melody and the left hand playing an accompaniment.

      Important composers:
    • Joseph Haydn (Austria, 1732 - 1809)
    • Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart (Austria, 1756 - 1791)
    • Ludwig van Beethoven (Germany, 1770 - 1827)

  3. The Romantic Period (1825 - 1900)

    Piano music of the Romantic Period was often written with long, beautiful melodies and complicated accompaniments that rely on the use of the pedal for legato.

      Important composers:
    • Franz Schubert
    • Robert Schumann (Germany, 1810 - 1856)
    • Frederic Chopin (b.Poland 1810 - d.France 1849)

  4. The 20th Century (1900 - 2000)

    Piano music of the 20th Century is written in many different styles.

      Important composers:
    • Bela Bartok (b.Hungary 1881 - d.New York 1945)
    • Dmitri Kabalevsky (Russia, 1904 - 1987)
